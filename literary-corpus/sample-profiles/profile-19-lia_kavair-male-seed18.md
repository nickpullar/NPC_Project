> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (702 TR)** — An incident in public damaged his name in the community.
> **Age 19 (703 TR)** — The other apprentice got the better tasks, the master's patience, the benefit of the doubt. He got the corrections and the night work. He learned both crafts — the craft itself, and the craft of being the one who is not the favourite.
> **Age 20 (704 TR)** — The master struck him across the ear for a piece of work that was not good enough. Not a severe beating — a correction, delivered with the flat of the hand and the specific economy of someone for whom this was a routine instructional tool rather than an expression of anger. The work had not been good enough. He accepted this. He also noted, privately, the specific quality of the master's expression: not angry, not sadistic, but impatient in the way of someone who expected better and was expressing that expectation physically because the verbal expression had not been sufficient. He made the work better. The master did not comment on the improvement.
> **Age 21 (705 TR)** — The purse is heavier than it looks. He can feel the specific weight of it through the lining of his coat, separate from his own coin, with its own gravity.
> **Age 22 (706 TR)** — He made a habit of it — the early mornings, the regular exertion. It accumulated.
> **Age 23 (707 TR)** — He was ill enough that people began making arrangements. He did not die, which surprised several of them. The illness left a lasting mark: stamina −1.
> **Age 24 (708 TR)** — They came on a Tuesday, two of them — one large, one polite. The polite one explained that the neighbourhood had been experiencing difficulties lately and that a modest arrangement would ensure his shop was not among those affected. He paid. He had a wife and an apprentice. He knew what refusing looked like.
> **Age 25 (709 TR)** — Shamed by his helplessness, he paid for lessons with a man-at-arms.
> **Age 27 (711 TR)** — He left the range of people who knew his name and spent several months in that condition, which suited him better than expected.
> **Age 28 (712 TR)** — He walked away from his field and did not look back. The town held something the village could not — or he had no choice but to find out.
> **Age 29 (713 TR)** — He struck up an acquaintance that would prove valuable in years to come. Made contact: Edric Paring (lia_kavair)
> **Age 30 (714 TR)** — The court found him guilty. What followed was swift and public.
> **Age 31 (715 TR)** — Someone he had trusted turned his confidence against him — for coin, for advantage, or from spite he never understood. Betrayed by Edric Paring
> **Age 32 (716 TR)** — Determined to regain his strength, he threw himself into physical discipline.
> **Age 33 (717 TR)** — He sweeps the workshop, prepares the materials, cleans the tools, sweeps the workshop again. This is approximately seventy percent of his current working life. The other thirty percent is the actual work, which he is only permitted to do under supervision and at the low end of the skill scale. He is learning to see the difference between the work and the work — between the surface activity and the underlying structure of the craft. He is not certain this perception is accurate. He is fairly certain that the sweeping is not going to stop anytime soon.
> **Age 34 (718 TR)** — The purse was gone before he reached for it. He had been in the market, in a crowd — a festival, probably, or a hanging-day when the press was thickest. He was annoyed rather than frightened. He knew the city well enough to know this was the cost of moving through it.
> **Age 35 (719 TR)** — Someone he knew — not a close friend, but a familiar face from the guild, the village, the neighbourhood — was killed. The world felt less safe after that.
> **Age 36 (720 TR)** — A friend — soldier, neighbour, or kin — died badly. The loss left a mark. Terath Parsen died
