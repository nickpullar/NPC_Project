> [!background] Background
> **Age 19 (692 TR)** — She entered her husband's household. The first months were an exercise in understanding the rules of a different world. Married Noral Cooper (artisan, age 25)
> **Age 19 (692 TR)** — She led her family confidently in entirely the wrong direction. They did not find this out until they were well off course. They were eventually fine.
> **Age 20 (693 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 20 (693 TR)** — The child came too still. She had carried it through the quickening, had felt it move, and then nothing. week 33
> **Age 21 (694 TR)** — She completed the commission and the merchant accepted it and paid the full rate. The merchant did not know she was the apprentice rather than the journeyman. She decided not to clarify this, which is an ethical position she has revisited several times since. Her conclusion: the work was good enough. The classification of who did it is a guild matter, not a customer matter. She told the master. The master said: correct.
> **Age 23 (696 TR)** — She did not know the woman was wanted. She knew the woman was frightened and had been asking careful questions about the road schedule and had paid a week's lodging in advance, which are each individually unremarkable and together suggest someone who expects to leave quickly. She noted this in her house book without acting on it. When the warrant arrived she cooperated completely with the alderman. She was not prosecuted. She thinks about it sometimes — the specific question of what she owed the woman versus what she owed the town — and has not reached a settled answer.
> **Age 23 (696 TR)** — She burned a dinner so completely that it became a family saying. Her spouse ate around it heroically.
> **Age 24 (697 TR)** — She ruined two yards of figured cloth that the master had been saving for a specific commission. The sound the mistress made when she saw it was not a word. She sat with that sound for a long time afterward — not out of guilt exactly, but out of the specific determination to understand precisely what had gone wrong at every stage of the decision and execution so that she could guarantee she never made those specific errors again. She can still describe all six errors in sequence. She will never make any of them.

She does the work. She also does the extra work. She does not complain about this. Complaining would be a third kind of work.
