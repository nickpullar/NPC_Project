> [!background] Background
> **Age 19 (716 TR)** — The journey required more of her than she had expected and gave back more than she had hoped.
> **Age 20 (717 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (718 TR)** — She bore a child. Vala Carter came into the world healthy. week 40 Vala Carter born (female)
> **Age 22 (719 TR)** — She was given in marriage, binding two households together. Married Rhoric Butcher (artisan, age 26)
> **Age 22 (719 TR)** — Her child produced a phrase so perfectly wrong that the whole household adopted it. Visitors were puzzled. The family didn't explain.
> **Age 23 (720 TR)** — She was sent to collect a pattern from the leatherworkers' guild house and waited while the guild officer found the document. She watched the cutting floor from the window. The leatherworkers had a different relationship with waste than her own trade — they cut for maximum material conservation, which produced a different kind of thinking about the pattern itself. She came back with the document and three observations she has been applying to her own practice.
