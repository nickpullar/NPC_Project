> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (716 TR)** — A door he expected to open did not. He pulled, then pushed, then pulled again, then read the situation correctly. There were witnesses.
> **Age 19 (717 TR)** — He reached for it out of habit — the third hook by the door, the left-hand drawer, the box that used to sit on the shelf — and found nothing. The absence is more present to him than the object ever was.
> **Age 20 (718 TR)** — He went somewhere that was muddy and came back in a state that was worse than he had anticipated.
> **Age 21 (719 TR)** — Something moved him to tears he had not expected. He was alone, which was fortunate.
> **Age 22 (720 TR)** — The hundred moot is held monthly and most months he has no business there. This month he does — a minor dispute about a field boundary that has been running since before he inherited the holding. He has the documentation. The other party also has documentation. The moot will look at both sets of documentation and reach a finding. He is not certain the finding will favour him. He has prepared an appeal to the shire assize on the assumption it won't, which is not pessimism but preparation. The moot is a public proceeding and everyone in the room knows everyone else and their business. This is simultaneously the moot's strength and its limitation.
