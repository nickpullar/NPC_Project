> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (693 TR)** — She travelled further from home than ever before, seeing new faces and hearing new tongues.
> **Age 19 (694 TR)** — She was who the village came to when there was no one else.
> **Age 20 (695 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (696 TR)** — She ruined two yards of figured cloth that the master had been saving for a specific commission. The sound the mistress made when she saw it was not a word. She sat with that sound for a long time afterward — not out of guilt exactly, but out of the specific determination to understand precisely what had gone wrong at every stage of the decision and execution so that she could guarantee she never made those specific errors again. She can still describe all six errors in sequence. She will never make any of them.
> **Age 22 (697 TR)** — She said something that was not true when the truth would have served equally well. She was puzzled by herself.
> **Age 23 (698 TR)** — The mission had been going well — that was the part that was hardest to hear. Twelve people who thought they understood the situation. She filed it under: the thing you assume is working is the thing that kills you.
> **Age 24 (699 TR)** — She read what texts she could find, and the night sky taught her the rest.
> **Age 25 (700 TR)** — Loss drove her inward; she spent months in solitary reflection.
> **Age 26 (701 TR)** — She wept at something small and ordinary. She was not embarrassed. She let it pass.
> **Age 27 (702 TR)** — She knelt and gave her answers. The Hesa-Eran bound her to the order. She had known since she was twelve it would come to this.
> **Age 28 (703 TR)** — Matakea. The full title. She had spent seven years as Ashesa working toward this and the ceremony itself was over in an afternoon.
> **Age 29 (704 TR)** — A military posting for a woman of the cloth was unusual. She received it as a mark of trust and declined to read it any other way.
> **Age 30 (705 TR)** — She went somewhere she had never been, which was unusual enough that she paid careful attention the entire time.
> **Age 31 (706 TR)** — She was called upon to speak where women rarely did — and was not found wanting.
> **Age 32 (707 TR)** — The other apprentice got the easier tasks, the better hours, the master's patience. She got the corrections and the night work and the jobs that were beneath notice if they went right and humiliating if they went wrong. She understood that this was partly preference and partly a test — the masters who treat their female apprentices harder are testing whether they will leave, because the easy ones always leave and the hard ones stay and become the best practitioners. She stayed. She is still deciding whether the theory justifies the practice.
> **Age 33 (708 TR)** — Determined to regain her strength, she threw herself into physical discipline.
> **Age 34 (709 TR)** — She noticed the lightness at her belt on the way home and knew. A market crowd, a moment of distraction. She had been careful. It had not been enough. She counted what she still had left and did not dwell on it.
> **Age 35 (710 TR)** — She was among the crowd when the sentence was carried out. The silence that fell at the end was not what she expected.
> **Age 36 (711 TR)** — A named posting — chaplain to a Lady of Paladins chapter. The knights watched her carefully for the first month to see how she would take the work.
> **Age 37 (712 TR)** — The year passed much as the last had.
> **Age 38 (713 TR)** — She walked to the shrine. The journey changed something — not in the way she expected, which was perhaps what it was supposed to do.
> **Age 39 (714 TR)** — Crisis drew her back to her faith with new fervour.
> **Age 40 (715 TR)** — She began writing — not because anyone asked, but because it needed doing.
> **Age 41 (716 TR)** — Months of close reading: liturgical texts, canonical precedent, the letters of the senior clergy. The work was slow and the illumination on the margins of the oldest manuscripts was occasionally distracting.
> **Age 42 (717 TR)** — A friend — companion, neighbour, or kin — died badly. The loss left a mark.
> **Age 43 (718 TR)** — She caught sight of something that was already past by the time she looked. She mentioned it to no one.
> **Age 44 (719 TR)** — Her childbearing years had passed. She felt the change in herself, and adapted.
> **Age 45 (720 TR)** — She would not have known him except that he laughed — the same short laugh, the kind that means nothing is funny. The cutpurse from the Endakil festival. She watched him move through the crowd with professional ease and felt something cold and very precise in her chest that was not exactly anger.
