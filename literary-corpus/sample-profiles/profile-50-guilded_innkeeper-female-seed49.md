> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (671 TR)** — A feud that began with a killing reached into her household. She had no say in the quarrel itself, but the consequences arrived at her door regardless. Feud with Torin Erman
> **Age 19 (672 TR)** — She did not know the woman was wanted. She knew the woman was frightened and had been asking careful questions about the road schedule and had paid a week's lodging in advance, which are each individually unremarkable and together suggest someone who expects to leave quickly. She noted this in her house book without acting on it. When the warrant arrived she cooperated completely with the alderman. She was not prosecuted. She thinks about it sometimes — the specific question of what she owed the woman versus what she owed the town — and has not reached a settled answer.
> **Age 20 (673 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (674 TR)** — She had trained her staff for exactly this eventuality, which she had always suspected was not a matter of if. The evacuation was clean. The loss was significant but not catastrophic: the taproom survived, the brewery survived, only the storage wing and the outer kitchen were gutted. She was trading again within the month. She had not been able to save the guest book, which annoyed her more than the structural damage.
> **Age 22 (675 TR)** — She rode out with hawk or hound when she could. The forest suited her.
> **Age 23 (676 TR)** — The morning begins with whatever the master left unfinished, which is the rule at every workshop and the reason early mornings in every workshop smell the same. She has been doing this for four years. She knows the rhythm of the place — which tasks come in sequence, which the master trusts her with unsupervised, which she is still not permitted to touch. The permitted list grows slowly. She measures her progress by what she is allowed to touch alone.
> **Age 25 (678 TR)** — The master put her hands on the material and showed her how the grain ran under the surface — not visually, by feel. She had been reading grain by eye. Reading it by touch produces different information. The two readings together produce a third kind of knowledge that she did not have before. She has been running her hands over every piece of material she touches since that afternoon, building the vocabulary.
> **Age 26 (679 TR)** — She worked alongside a artisan through a season, watching and helping.
> **Age 29 (682 TR)** — A man of means noticed her. What followed was not love, precisely, but it was not nothing either. She had a room that was warm and food that was regular, and she understood the terms.
> **Age 30 (683 TR)** — He made it legitimate. She had not let herself believe he would. The wedding was modest and the gossip was fierce and she did not care about either.
> **Age 31 (684 TR)** — The other apprentice got the easier tasks, the better hours, the master's patience. She got the corrections and the night work and the jobs that were beneath notice if they went right and humiliating if they went wrong. She understood that this was partly preference and partly a test — the masters who treat their female apprentices harder are testing whether they will leave, because the easy ones always leave and the hard ones stay and become the best practitioners. She stayed. She is still deciding whether the theory justifies the practice.
> **Age 32 (685 TR)** — She said something that was not true when the truth would have served equally well. She was puzzled by herself.
> **Age 33 (686 TR)** — She travelled further from home than ever before, seeing new faces and hearing new tongues.
> **Age 34 (687 TR)** — The lock had been forced cleanly. They had known what they were looking for. Her coin box, the good cloth, the thing her mother had left her. She found herself straightening objects that had been moved and then stopped because she could not stop thinking about the hands that had moved them.
> **Age 35 (688 TR)** — The child lived. She could see from the beginning that they would need more — more care, more patience, more of everything she had. She did not hesitate. week 40 — deaf Karith Morsen born (female, deaf)
> **Age 35 (688 TR)** — Her child asked her what happened after death. She gave the best answer she had. She was not sure it was good enough.
> **Age 36 (689 TR)** — She saw a situation developing that she might have done something about. She walked on. The question of whether she was right to has not fully settled.
> **Age 37 (690 TR)** — She heard the cry and had the choice that women always have in these moments: whether joining would help or whether she would be more useful as a witness, which is a calculation she makes in approximately half a second. She positioned herself where she could see the man being pursued, watched where he went, and when the officers arrived she described his route and his description clearly and they found him two streets over. She was thanked by the bailiff. She has been a useful witness in two similar situations. She considers this a form of civic participation she is good at.
> **Age 37 (690 TR)** — She caught herself watching the child too long, calculating futures. She stopped herself and went back to work. The watching started again an hour later.

> **Age 41 (694 TR)** — She gave to the church or the poor. It mattered to her that it was done.

> **Age 47 (700 TR)** — Serving on the assize jury is not common for women but her holding qualifies her and the bailiff called her. She went. She listened more carefully than most jurors she observed, because she was aware that her presence was being watched by everyone in the room and that any appearance of confusion would be attributed to her sex rather than to the complexity of the case. The cases were not complex. She returned a clear verdict in each. The bailiff thanked her with the specific tone of a man who was expecting difficulty and didn't get it.
> **Age 47 (700 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 47 (700 TR)** — She lost Karith Morsen — the deaf child she had given so much to. Karith Morsen died (age 12)
> **Age 48 (701 TR)** — She forgot an observance she had kept every year of her adult life. She was not sure what that meant.
> **Age 49 (702 TR)** — She brought the complaint herself, which is her right as a freeholder. The lord's steward had assessed her labour obligation incorrectly and she had the documents to show it. She presented them clearly and without elaboration. The lord looked at the documents and corrected the assessment. She thanked him in the appropriate form and left. The whole proceeding took perhaps half an hour. She noted afterward that the lord had been fair and prompt and had not required her to argue for something the documents already demonstrated. She has a higher opinion of this particular lord than she expected to have.
> **Age 50 (703 TR)** — The Trail was Pagaelin territory in every practical sense regardless of what the maps said. She learned its rhythms — which stretches drew attention, which seasons were dangerous, which small gestures communicated respect without communicating weakness.
> **Age 51 (704 TR)** — She handed the accounts and keys to a younger hand and stepped back.
> **Age 52 (705 TR)** — She went somewhere she had never been, which was unusual enough that she paid careful attention the entire time.
> **Age 53 (706 TR)** — The franchise had come available when old Maret died with no heirs. She had moved faster than the men who assumed she would not, and had the backing of two syndics before the others had finished shaking their heads. The vote was closer than she liked. She did not let this show.
> **Age 54 (707 TR)** — She chose to end the enmity. It cost her pride and gained her something she could not name. Reconciled with Kern Deepsack
> **Age 55 (708 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.

She will say exactly what she knows and nothing she doesn't know and she will be as clear as possible about which is which. The moot's ability to get this right depends on witnesses being honest. She intends to be an example of what that looks like.
