> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (704 TR)** — The sky held patterns any farmer learned to read. He had finally learned to read them.
> **Age 19 (705 TR)** — He made a habit of it — the early mornings, the regular exertion. It accumulated.

> **Age 21 (707 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 22 (708 TR)** — He heard a song at a market and could not stop hearing it for three months. He did not know the words.
> **Age 23 (709 TR)** — Brigands relieved him of coin and dignity alike.
> **Age 24 (710 TR)** — A crow appeared every morning for a month on the same post. He began to look for it. One day it was gone.
> **Age 25 (711 TR)** — He struck up an acquaintance that would prove valuable in years to come. Made contact: Vala Alendyl (noble)
> **Age 26 (712 TR)** — He recovered by degrees, marking progress in what he could do that he had not managed the week before.
> **Age 27 (713 TR)** — Necessity made him the nearest thing to a healer in his community.
> **Age 28 (714 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 29 (715 TR)** — The shout went up from the market quarter and he was close enough that joining was not optional — the custom is that any man who hears it and does not join is liable to be treated as an accomplice. He ran with the others for three streets before they cornered the man in a yard. The man had stolen a purse. He held one arm while the officers bound him. The whole thing lasted perhaps ten minutes. He thought about it afterward: the specific way the crowd becomes a legal instrument when the right words are shouted, and how different it felt to be part of that instrument than he had expected it to feel.
> **Age 30 (716 TR)** — His father died and the lord's right to heriot was immediate: the best beast, or a cash equivalent if you preferred to keep the beast. He paid in cash. The amount was calculated by the reeve against the estate valuation and he disputed two of the three items in the valuation and settled the third because it was correct. The payment took everything he had saved in the previous two years. He is now the effective head of the family holding and has begun saving again. The death tax on a freeman's estate is not technically death — it is the lord's way of reminding you that the arrangement is contractual and the contract has terms.
> **Age 31 (717 TR)** — Named outlaw by a lord or court. Travel to markets and towns carried new risk.
> **Age 32 (718 TR)** — He drank more than he intended at a gathering and said things he would not have said sober. He reviewed these the next day at length.
> **Age 33 (719 TR)** — The disaster came without warning. What had taken years to build was gone in hours.
> **Age 34 (720 TR)** — It had not started as enmity. By the end, it was nothing else. Vala Alendyl became an enemy
