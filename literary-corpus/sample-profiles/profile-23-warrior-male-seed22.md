> **Age 28 (708 TR)** — He had the broadsword because he found it, which is not a defence. He carried it for two months because he needed a sword and it was a good sword and he told himself no one would notice. Someone noticed. He was brought before the hundred moot on a charge of breach of rank: carrying chivalric arms without the legal right to do so. The moot fined him, confiscated the sword, and issued a formal warning that a repeat offence would carry corporal punishment. He paid the fine. He has carried a shortsword since, which is his right, and which he is careful to carry visibly so that there is no question about what it is.
> **Age 29 (709 TR)** — The Peonian sister arrived before he had finished sending for her and left after dawn with the kind of professional calm that made everything that had happened in between feel almost ordinary. He does not know her name. He knows the child is alive.
