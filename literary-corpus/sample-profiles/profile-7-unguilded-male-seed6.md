> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (714 TR)** — A crow appeared every morning for a month on the same post. He began to look for it. One day it was gone.
> **Age 19 (715 TR)** — The journey took him far enough from home that home became an abstraction he could think about rather than simply inhabit.
> **Age 20 (716 TR)** — The shout went up from the market quarter and he was close enough that joining was not optional — the custom is that any man who hears it and does not join is liable to be treated as an accomplice. He ran with the others for three streets before they cornered the man in a yard. The man had stolen a purse. He held one arm while the officers bound him. The whole thing lasted perhaps ten minutes. He thought about it afterward: the specific way the crowd becomes a legal instrument when the right words are shouted, and how different it felt to be part of that instrument than he had expected it to feel.
> **Age 21 (717 TR)** — The crop rotted or the rains came late. Winter would be lean.
> **Age 22 (718 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 23 (719 TR)** — He took a wrong turn and came out somewhere he had never been. The village had a good bakehouse and the wrong road became one he always took.
> **Age 24 (720 TR)** — The sky held patterns any farmer learned to read. He had finally learned to read them.
