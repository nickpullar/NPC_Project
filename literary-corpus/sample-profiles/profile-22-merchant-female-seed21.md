> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (699 TR)** — She watched someone who really knew what they were doing. She had thought she knew. She saw now that she had been adequate.
> **Age 19 (700 TR)** — She heard the cry and had the choice that women always have in these moments: whether joining would help or whether she would be more useful as a witness, which is a calculation she makes in approximately half a second. She positioned herself where she could see the man being pursued, watched where he went, and when the officers arrived she described his route and his description clearly and they found him two streets over. She was thanked by the bailiff. She has been a useful witness in two similar situations. She considers this a form of civic participation she is good at.
> **Age 20 (701 TR)** — Crisis drew her back to her faith with new fervour.
> **Age 21 (702 TR)** — She said something that was not true when the truth would have served equally well. She was puzzled by herself.
> **Age 22 (703 TR)** — She entered her husband's household. The first months were an exercise in understanding the rules of a different world. Married Harlan Wainworth (merchant, age 26)
> **Age 22 (703 TR)** — The child came too still. She had carried it through the quickening, had felt it move, and then nothing. week 35
> **Age 23 (704 TR)** — She struck up an acquaintance that would prove valuable in years to come. Made contact: Ruald Deepsack (merchant)
> **Age 23 (704 TR)** — Something became funny in a way that built and built until everyone was helpless with it. She remembered it for the rest of her life without being able to say what exactly had been funny.
> **Age 24 (705 TR)** — She accompanied a caravan — widow, factor, or simply bold — to distant markets.
> **Age 25 (706 TR)** — She let a coin decide. It came down one way. Her reaction told her which way she had hoped it would land.
> **Age 25 (706 TR)** — She lost the child early — before she had said much aloud about it. She said nothing after, either. week 6
> **Age 26 (707 TR)** — She bought cheap and sold dear, and the ledger proved it.
> **Age 26 (707 TR)** — She lost the child before its time. She did not speak of it openly. week 14
> **Age 27 (708 TR)** — The Trail was Pagaelin territory in every practical sense regardless of what the maps said. She learned its rhythms — which stretches drew attention, which seasons were dangerous, which small gestures communicated respect without communicating weakness.
> **Age 27 (708 TR)** — Her first child arrived. Rarith Wainworth. She had not understood, until the moment it was placed in her arms, how much of herself she would give to it. week 40 Rarith Wainworth born (female)
> **Age 28 (709 TR)** — She grieved thoroughly and privately. When she came through it she was quieter than she had been, and harder to read.
> **Age 28 (709 TR)** — She bore a child. Kela Wainworth came into the world healthy. week 40 Kela Wainworth born (female)
> **Age 29 (710 TR)** — She went somewhere she had never been, which was unusual enough that she paid careful attention the entire time.
> **Age 30 (711 TR)** — She carries a knife. Every woman with any sense carries a knife. A guardsman at the town gate told her knives were not permitted in the market quarter without a licence. She asked him to show her the ordinance. He could not produce it. She waited. He eventually admitted he had misremembered. She thanked him and entered the market quarter. She noted the guardsman's name in her memory. A week later she checked the town charter at the guild house. There is no such ordinance. There never was.
> **Age 31 (712 TR)** — Crisis drew her back to her faith with new fervour.
> **Age 32 (713 TR)** — She bought cheap and sold dear, and the ledger proved it.
> **Age 32 (713 TR)** — The child was lost before it had become real to anyone else. She marked it privately. week 8
> **Age 33 (714 TR)** — She dreamed something that she decided not to speak of. She has never spoken of it.
> **Age 33 (714 TR)** — Her child produced a phrase so perfectly wrong that the whole household adopted it. Visitors were puzzled. The family didn't explain.
> **Age 34 (715 TR)** — Through persistence or her late husband's name, she won a place in the guild.
> **Age 34 (715 TR)** — She found something her husband had kept that she had not known about. It was ordinary. She was more curious about why he had kept it than about the thing itself.
> **Age 35 (716 TR)** — He endured. The details were different; the shape was the same.
