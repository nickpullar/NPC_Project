> **Age 21 (711 TR)** — He saw a man die by violence — not on a battlefield, but in the ordinary world: a tavern, a street, a market square. It changed how he looked at strangers.
> **Age 21 (711 TR)** — The child lived, but it was plain from the first days that something was different. He loved the child completely and did not know what the future held. week 40 — lame Arith Edsen born (female, lame)
> **Age 22 (712 TR)** — The Argent Order knight identified the man as Navehan, which explained several things about the last two weeks. He killed him in the tunnel mouth with a hand-axe in less than thirty seconds. The killing felt different from others he had done. Not better, exactly — but more contained, more purposeful. He did not examine this feeling very closely.
> **Age 22 (712 TR)** — He walked somewhere with his spouse or a child and they said almost nothing the whole way. It was one of the better journeys he remembered.
> **Age 23 (713 TR)** — The lord held court in the hall and he was summoned as a party to a dispute about labour obligations. The lord sat in the chair at the end of the hall and heard both sides in the specific way that lords hear disputes: with one eye on the merits and one eye on whether the outcome will produce two functional tenants or one resentful one. The finding went against him on the main point and in his favour on the secondary point, which is a finding designed to ensure that both parties leave with something. He accepted it. The lord's court is not impartial — the lord is also his landlord — but it is not arbitrary either. It is interested, which is a different thing.
> **Age 23 (713 TR)** — His wife found something he had kept from before they met — a letter, a token, an old drawing — and asked about it. He told her the truth. It was not interesting. She had expected it to be interesting.
> **Age 24 (714 TR)** — He ran the Genin Trail often enough that the same faces appeared twice, then again. Some demanded tolls. Some offered goods — often very good goods, origin unasked. A few just watched from the ridge and let him pass. He learned which behaviour meant which thing.
> **Age 24 (714 TR)** — The child came, and with it something he had not prepared for — an attachment that rearranged everything else. Beren Edsen. week 40 Beren Edsen born (male)
> **Age 25 (715 TR)** — He told a story at a campfire that the others repeated for years. He had mostly made it up.
> **Age 25 (715 TR)** — One of his children overheard a conversation and formed a conclusion so wrong it took months to correct. By then the wrong version had spread to the neighbours.
> **Age 26 (716 TR)** — He reached the temple doors ahead of the men pursuing him and the priest let him in. Whether it was the law or simply that the priest had seen his face and made a judgment, he was never certain. He stayed three days on a pallet in the vestibule and left by the back when they told him it was clear.
> **Age 26 (716 TR)** — He endured. The details were different; the shape was the same.
> **Age 27 (717 TR)** — Steel found him. The wound healed, after a fashion.
> **Age 27 (717 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 27 (717 TR)** — The year passed much as the last had.
> **Age 28 (718 TR)** — His reliability and competence were noticed. Rank followed.
> **Age 28 (718 TR)** — He lay awake thinking about what would happen to the child after he was gone. There was no answer he could land on.
> **Age 29 (719 TR)** — The injury was too deep. He would never move as freely again. A grievous wound to the shoulder. The damage was permanent — strength reduced by 3.
> **Age 30 (720 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
