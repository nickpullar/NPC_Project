> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (713 TR)** — She completed the commission and the merchant accepted it and paid the full rate. The merchant did not know she was the apprentice rather than the journeyman. She decided not to clarify this, which is an ethical position she has revisited several times since. Her conclusion: the work was good enough. The classification of who did it is a guild matter, not a customer matter. She told the master. The master said: correct.
> **Age 19 (714 TR)** — She struck up an acquaintance that would prove valuable in years to come. Made contact: Arith (clergy)
> **Age 20 (715 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (716 TR)** — She watched the mistress take apart a piece she had thought was finished and rebuild the central section from scratch in forty minutes. It was the same result, technically — the dimensions matched, the specifications were met. But the new version had something the first version had not had, something she could see when they were side by side but cannot yet name precisely. The mistress saw her looking. She did not explain. She said: when you can say what the difference is, you are ready for the examination.
> **Age 22 (717 TR)** — She borrowed something meaning to return it. That was years ago. It has become hers through inertia.
> **Age 23 (718 TR)** — She passed a room in the night and saw light underneath the door. She paused and then went on. She asked about it in the morning.
> **Age 24 (719 TR)** — Dancing was expected of her from childhood. She was accomplished.
> **Age 25 (720 TR)** — She saw something in the grain of a beam — a face, a figure, a landscape — and noted it and went on.
