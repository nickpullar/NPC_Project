> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (695 TR)** — She is the only woman in the workshop. This is a fact with specific texture: not hostile, exactly, but requiring a level of navigation that the male apprentices do not perform. She has worked out what is expected of her in addition to the craft itself — how much social work is required, where the lines are, which of the journeymen are worth the investment and which are looking for a reason to be difficult. She does the work. She also does the extra work. She does not complain about this. Complaining would be a third kind of work.
> **Age 19 (696 TR)** — She was sent to collect a pattern from the leatherworkers' guild house and waited while the guild officer found the document. She watched the cutting floor from the window. The leatherworkers had a different relationship with waste than her own trade — they cut for maximum material conservation, which produced a different kind of thinking about the pattern itself. She came back with the document and three observations she has been applying to her own practice.
> **Age 20 (697 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (698 TR)** — She ruined two yards of figured cloth that the master had been saving for a specific commission. The sound the mistress made when she saw it was not a word. She sat with that sound for a long time afterward — not out of guilt exactly, but out of the specific determination to understand precisely what had gone wrong at every stage of the decision and execution so that she could guarantee she never made those specific errors again. She can still describe all six errors in sequence. She will never make any of them.
> **Age 22 (699 TR)** — The morning begins with whatever the master left unfinished, which is the rule at every workshop and the reason early mornings in every workshop smell the same. She has been doing this for four years. She knows the rhythm of the place — which tasks come in sequence, which the master trusts her with unsupervised, which she is still not permitted to touch. The permitted list grows slowly. She measures her progress by what she is allowed to touch alone.
> **Age 23 (700 TR)** — The chemical burned a stripe across the back of her wrist when the vessel cracked unexpectedly. She poured water over it immediately, which was the right response, and the burn is less bad than it might have been. She knows now what the material does under certain conditions, and she knows it in a way that reading about it did not produce. The wrist heals. The knowledge stays.
