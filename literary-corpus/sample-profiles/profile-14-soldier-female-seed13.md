> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (707 TR)** — Necessity had long since made her skilled. She went beyond necessity.
> **Age 19 (708 TR)** — She identified it as camp fever on the second day and began the isolation protocols that the army officer had not heard of and was initially resistant to. She explained clearly why isolation was necessary. He allowed it after two days. Those two days probably cost two of the seven men who died. She has thought about how to explain this more quickly next time, which is the productive direction for the thought, and she returns to it when she is not too tired to think productively.
> **Age 20 (709 TR)** — The camp economy runs on the sutlers and everyone knows it, including the officers who would prefer not to acknowledge it. She extends credit carefully — who is reliable, who has family money, who is the kind of man who runs when things go wrong. She has been cheated precisely twice and recovered from both by knowing which sergeant to speak to. She is carrying the wages of four dead men in her ledger, which she will attempt to recover from their estates when the campaign ends, and which she is fairly certain she will not recover.

> **Age 24 (713 TR)** — Brigands relieved her of coin and dignity alike.
> **Age 25 (714 TR)** — She kept to it when most would have stopped. The discipline became its own reward.
> **Age 26 (715 TR)** — She opened what she thought was the right door. It was not. She apologised to the people inside and withdrew.
> **Age 27 (716 TR)** — She was asked to deliver a package to a specific house in a town sixty miles out and note, on the way back, what she could observe about the movement of men and horses on the Tashal road. She delivered the package. She observed the movement. Her report contained three pieces of information her officer had not expected, which she knows because of how he reacted to those three pieces. She is aware she is useful in a specific way. She is deciding what to do with that awareness.
> **Age 28 (717 TR)** — She was not devout. She went to the Matakea the night before the assault because several people she respected had done the same, and because the mathematics of the next day were not reassuring. She did not find God in the conversation. She found something else — a clarity about what she was willing to die for, which turned out to be enough.
> **Age 29 (718 TR)** — He endured. The details were different; the shape was the same.
> **Age 30 (719 TR)** — The Trail was Pagaelin territory in every practical sense regardless of what the maps said. She learned its rhythms — which stretches drew attention, which seasons were dangerous, which small gestures communicated respect without communicating weakness.
> **Age 31 (720 TR)** — She saw a situation developing that she might have done something about. She walked on. The question of whether she was right to has not fully settled.
