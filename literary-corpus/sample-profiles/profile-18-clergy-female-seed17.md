> [!background] Background

> **Age 19 (704 TR)** — The morning begins with whatever the master left unfinished, which is the rule at every workshop and the reason early mornings in every workshop smell the same. She has been doing this for four years. She knows the rhythm of the place — which tasks come in sequence, which the master trusts her with unsupervised, which she is still not permitted to touch. The permitted list grows slowly. She measures her progress by what she is allowed to touch alone.
> **Age 20 (705 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
