> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (683 TR)** — She was who the village came to when there was no one else.
> **Age 19 (684 TR)** — Music was part of a proper upbringing. She took the lessons seriously.
> **Age 20 (685 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (686 TR)** — She thought of what she should have said long after there was any chance to say it.
> **Age 22 (687 TR)** — She came back to herself slowly, and not entirely the same self that had been taken down.
> **Age 23 (688 TR)** — She carries a knife. Every woman with any sense carries a knife. A guardsman at the town gate told her knives were not permitted in the market quarter without a licence. She asked him to show her the ordinance. He could not produce it. She waited. He eventually admitted he had misremembered. She thanked him and entered the market quarter. She noted the guardsman's name in her memory. A week later she checked the town charter at the guild house. There is no such ordinance. There never was.
> **Age 24 (689 TR)** — She ruined two yards of figured cloth that the master had been saving for a specific commission. The sound the mistress made when she saw it was not a word. She sat with that sound for a long time afterward — not out of guilt exactly, but out of the specific determination to understand precisely what had gone wrong at every stage of the decision and execution so that she could guarantee she never made those specific errors again. She can still describe all six errors in sequence. She will never make any of them.
> **Age 25 (690 TR)** — She bought cheap and sold dear, and the ledger proved it.
> **Age 26 (691 TR)** — A woman she knew was killed — a neighbour, a trader she dealt with, someone whose face she knew. It was the kind of death that made everyone quieter for a while.
> **Age 27 (692 TR)** — She heard the midnight hour ring from somewhere she had never been. She lay awake listening to the silence after.
> **Age 28 (693 TR)** — She watched the mistress take apart a piece she had thought was finished and rebuild the central section from scratch in forty minutes. It was the same result, technically — the dimensions matched, the specifications were met. But the new version had something the first version had not had, something she could see when they were side by side but cannot yet name precisely. The mistress saw her looking. She did not explain. She said: when you can say what the difference is, you are ready for the examination.
