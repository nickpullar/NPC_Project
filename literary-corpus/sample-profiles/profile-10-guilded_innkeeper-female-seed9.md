> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (711 TR)** — She would not have known him except that he laughed — the same short laugh, the kind that means nothing is funny. The cutpurse from the Endakil festival. She watched him move through the crowd with professional ease and felt something cold and very precise in her chest that was not exactly anger.
> **Age 19 (712 TR)** — The guild expected journeymen to travel and she had obliged, moving on when she had taken what a workshop had to teach. Arriving unknown in a new settlement and finding the master's shop, presenting herself, negotiating her rate — she had done it eleven times and had grown fluent in the particular conversation of a artisan offering her skills to a stranger.
> **Age 20 (713 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (714 TR)** — She stood in the ruins and decided what came next.
> **Age 22 (715 TR)** — A quarrel turned violent; she left someone badly hurt.
> **Age 23 (716 TR)** — Necessity had long since made her skilled. She went beyond necessity.
> **Age 24 (717 TR)** — The other apprentice got the easier tasks, the better hours, the master's patience. She got the corrections and the night work and the jobs that were beneath notice if they went right and humiliating if they went wrong. She understood that this was partly preference and partly a test — the masters who treat their female apprentices harder are testing whether they will leave, because the easy ones always leave and the hard ones stay and become the best practitioners. She stayed. She is still deciding whether the theory justifies the practice.
> **Age 25 (718 TR)** — She did not know the woman was wanted. She knew the woman was frightened and had been asking careful questions about the road schedule and had paid a week's lodging in advance, which are each individually unremarkable and together suggest someone who expects to leave quickly. She noted this in her house book without acting on it. When the warrant arrived she cooperated completely with the alderman. She was not prosecuted. She thinks about it sometimes — the specific question of what she owed the woman versus what she owed the town — and has not reached a settled answer.
> **Age 26 (719 TR)** — The party arrived in the evening — four men, armed, carrying themselves like soldiers but wearing nothing identifying. She gave them the south room with two beds and told the kitchen to send up hot food. She has been running this inn for twelve years and she knows exactly what these men are and what they are doing in her inn, which is transiting from somewhere to somewhere on business that is not her business, and the correct response to that is: good rooms, good food, prompt service, correct change, and a very accurate memory of faces.
