> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (701 TR)** — She is the only woman in the workshop. This is a fact with specific texture: not hostile, exactly, but requiring a level of navigation that the male apprentices do not perform. She has worked out what is expected of her in addition to the craft itself — how much social work is required, where the lines are, which of the journeymen are worth the investment and which are looking for a reason to be difficult. She does the work. She also does the extra work. She does not complain about this. Complaining would be a third kind of work.
> **Age 19 (702 TR)** — She could tell rain from cloud before most felt the wind shift.
> **Age 20 (703 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (704 TR)** — She entered her husband's household. The first months were an exercise in understanding the rules of a different world. Married Gorin Daling (guilded_innkeeper, age 26)
> **Age 21 (704 TR)** — The birth took longer than expected. Elva Daling lived. She spent the next three days learning who she now was. week 40 Elva Daling born (female)
> **Age 22 (705 TR)** — She had trained her staff for exactly this eventuality, which she had always suspected was not a matter of if. The evacuation was clean. The loss was significant but not catastrophic: the taproom survived, the brewery survived, only the storage wing and the outer kitchen were gutted. She was trading again within the month. She had not been able to save the guest book, which annoyed her more than the structural damage.
> **Age 22 (705 TR)** — Something became funny in a way that built and built until everyone was helpless with it. She remembered it for the rest of her life without being able to say what exactly had been funny.
> **Age 23 (706 TR)** — The death of a relative brought unexpected property or coin into her hands.
> **Age 23 (706 TR)** — Another loss before it had become a child in any way others would recognise. She grieved it nonetheless. week 15
> **Age 24 (707 TR)** — The last guest has been seen to their room. The accounts balance to the Farthing. She is standing in the common room with the fire going low and the room showing what seventeen people did in it today. This is hers: the room, the inn, the daily enterprise of feeding and sheltering and managing the specific parade of humanity that comes through here. She is tired in the way she is always tired at this hour, which is to say: satisfied and ready for tomorrow.
> **Age 24 (707 TR)** — She found something her husband had kept that she had not known about. It was ordinary. She was more curious about why he had kept it than about the thing itself.
> **Age 25 (708 TR)** — The morning begins with whatever the master left unfinished, which is the rule at every workshop and the reason early mornings in every workshop smell the same. She has been doing this for four years. She knows the rhythm of the place — which tasks come in sequence, which the master trusts her with unsupervised, which she is still not permitted to touch. The permitted list grows slowly. She measures her progress by what she is allowed to touch alone.
> **Age 26 (709 TR)** — She travelled further from home than ever before, seeing new faces and hearing new tongues.
> **Age 26 (709 TR)** — She discovered her husband had a skill she had never known about. She had lived with him for years. She watched him demonstrate it with quiet pride.
> **Age 27 (710 TR)** — She was who the village came to when there was no one else.
> **Age 27 (710 TR)** — Her child produced a phrase so perfectly wrong that the whole household adopted it. Visitors were puzzled. The family didn't explain.
> **Age 28 (711 TR)** — She did not know the woman was wanted. She knew the woman was frightened and had been asking careful questions about the road schedule and had paid a week's lodging in advance, which are each individually unremarkable and together suggest someone who expects to leave quickly. She noted this in her house book without acting on it. When the warrant arrived she cooperated completely with the alderman. She was not prosecuted. She thinks about it sometimes — the specific question of what she owed the woman versus what she owed the town — and has not reached a settled answer.
> **Age 28 (711 TR)** — She bore a child. Edric Daling came into the world healthy. week 40 Edric Daling born (male)
> **Age 29 (712 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 29 (712 TR)** — He endured. The details were different; the shape was the same.
> **Age 31 (714 TR)** — The woman died in room four. She had arrived alone, which was itself notable. The alderman came the same morning and she walked him through everything she knew: arrival time, manner, payment, whether she had spoken to anyone, whether anyone had enquired after her. The alderman asked whether she thought it was natural causes. She said she did not know and had not formed an opinion and suggested that was his job. The inquiry concluded accidental death. She does not entirely agree with the conclusion but she has said everything she knows and has nothing further to add to the record.
> **Age 32 (715 TR)** — She rode out with hawk or hound when she could. The forest suited her.
> **Age 32 (715 TR)** — Her husband snored like something structural was collapsing. She learned to sleep through it. She never told him how long this took.
> **Age 34 (717 TR)** — She noticed the lightness at her belt on the way home and knew. A market crowd, a moment of distraction. She had been careful. It had not been enough. She counted what she still had left and did not dwell on it.
> **Age 34 (717 TR)** — Her child fell into the water. She was in after them before she thought about it. They were both soaked and frightened and unhurt.
> **Age 35 (718 TR)** — She completed the commission and the merchant accepted it and paid the full rate. The merchant did not know she was the apprentice rather than the journeyman. She decided not to clarify this, which is an ethical position she has revisited several times since. Her conclusion: the work was good enough. The classification of who did it is a guild matter, not a customer matter. She told the master. The master said: correct.
> **Age 36 (719 TR)** — She had paid dues in bad years before. This year was different: the new baby, the tools she had replaced, the roof. She asked the chapter for a loan and was told to present her case to the syndics, which she did, in writing, which she had heard was more effective. The syndics granted the deferral for six months. She paid it back in five.
> **Age 36 (719 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 36 (719 TR)** — She lost Edric Daling — still a child, not yet grown. Edric Daling died (age 8)
> **Age 37 (720 TR)** — A fever kept her bedridden for weeks; she emerged gaunt but alive. The illness passed without lasting harm.
> **Age 37 (720 TR)** — Her child carried something home — a dog, a cat, a bird with a broken wing — that no one had said yes to. It lived in the house for a long time.
