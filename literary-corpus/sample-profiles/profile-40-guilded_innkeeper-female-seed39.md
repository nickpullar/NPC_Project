> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (681 TR)** — The woman died in room four. She had arrived alone, which was itself notable. The alderman came the same morning and she walked him through everything she knew: arrival time, manner, payment, whether she had spoken to anyone, whether anyone had enquired after her. The alderman asked whether she thought it was natural causes. She said she did not know and had not formed an opinion and suggested that was his job. The inquiry concluded accidental death. She does not entirely agree with the conclusion but she has said everything she knows and has nothing further to add to the record.
> **Age 19 (682 TR)** — Someone had told the Matakea that she had been seen at a location associated with Navehan activity. She had not been there. The interview was thorough. She was believed, eventually. She spent some time afterward identifying who might have made the report and why.
> **Age 20 (683 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (684 TR)** — The Matakea heard her story and asked no questions about whether it was complete. She was given a room in the lay quarters and told she could stay until she decided what she was doing. That was two weeks, and during that time no one came for her.
> **Age 22 (685 TR)** — A fever kept her bedridden for weeks; she emerged gaunt but alive. The illness passed without lasting harm.
> **Age 24 (687 TR)** — She was who the village came to when there was no one else.
> **Age 25 (688 TR)** — The guild expected journeymen to travel and she had obliged, moving on when she had taken what a workshop had to teach. Arriving unknown in a new settlement and finding the master's shop, presenting herself, negotiating her rate — she had done it eleven times and had grown fluent in the particular conversation of a artisan offering her skills to a stranger.
> **Age 26 (689 TR)** — She did not know the woman was wanted. She knew the woman was frightened and had been asking careful questions about the road schedule and had paid a week's lodging in advance, which are each individually unremarkable and together suggest someone who expects to leave quickly. She noted this in her house book without acting on it. When the warrant arrived she cooperated completely with the alderman. She was not prosecuted. She thinks about it sometimes — the specific question of what she owed the woman versus what she owed the town — and has not reached a settled answer.
> **Age 27 (690 TR)** — She rode out with hawk or hound when she could. The forest suited her.
> **Age 28 (691 TR)** — The magistrate heard her case and found against her, which surprised her until she learned what the other party had paid. She had known in the abstract that the law was purchasable in this city. She knew it differently after that.
> **Age 29 (692 TR)** — She kept the household alive through ingenuity and stubbornness.
> **Age 31 (694 TR)** — Crisis drew her back to her faith with new fervour.
> **Age 32 (695 TR)** — The mark was permanent — ear, thumb, or cheek. She would carry it the rest of her life.
> **Age 34 (697 TR)** — She travelled further from home than ever before, seeing new faces and hearing new tongues.
> **Age 35 (698 TR)** — She noticed the lightness at her belt on the way home and knew. A market crowd, a moment of distraction. She had been careful. It had not been enough. She counted what she still had left and did not dwell on it.
> **Age 37 (700 TR)** — The investigation was about whether she was operating a house of assignment in the upper rooms — a serious accusation that, if proven, would cost her the inn charter. She was not. She cooperated fully, allowed a complete inspection, produced the house book going back four years, and invited the alderman to speak with any current or former guest he could locate. The investigation closed with no finding. She knows who made the allegation and why. She has not acted on this knowledge and does not intend to. The record is clear. That is sufficient.
> **Age 38 (701 TR)** — She fought for what was hers through every channel available to her.
> **Age 39 (702 TR)** — She does not sit in the common room after the dinner hour. She is present in it — moving between tables, checking the fire, signalling to the kitchen — but she is not stationary in it, because a stationary innkeeper becomes a feature of the room and people stop noticing her, and she wants to be noticed. Not as a presence that changes behaviour, but as a person who is paying attention. The room behaves differently when it knows someone is watching. It behaves better.
> **Age 40 (703 TR)** — She struck up an acquaintance that would prove valuable in years to come. Made contact: Nara Ering (guilded_innkeeper)
> **Age 41 (704 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.

> **Age 44 (707 TR)** — Her childbearing years had passed. She felt the change in herself, and adapted.
> **Age 45 (708 TR)** — She had learned to be careful. This time she had not been careful enough. The look on the stranger's face told her everything.
> **Age 46 (709 TR)** — Crisis drew her back to her faith with new fervour.
> **Age 47 (710 TR)** — The last guest has been seen to their room. The accounts balance to the Farthing. She is standing in the common room with the fire going low and the room showing what seventeen people did in it today. This is hers: the room, the inn, the daily enterprise of feeding and sheltering and managing the specific parade of humanity that comes through here. She is tired in the way she is always tired at this hour, which is to say: satisfied and ready for tomorrow.
