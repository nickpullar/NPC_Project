> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (673 TR)** — She repaired something — a step, a latch, a cracked fitting — that people had walked past broken for so long they had stopped seeing it.
> **Age 19 (674 TR)** — She came across someone sleeping where they should not have been. She covered them with something and went away.
> **Age 20 (675 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (676 TR)** — Refusing to be a victim again, she found someone willing to teach her.
> **Age 22 (677 TR)** — She walked to the shrine. The journey changed something — not in the way she expected, which was perhaps what it was supposed to do.
> **Age 23 (678 TR)** — She carries a knife. Every woman with any sense carries a knife. A guardsman at the town gate told her knives were not permitted in the market quarter without a licence. She asked him to show her the ordinance. He could not produce it. She waited. He eventually admitted he had misremembered. She thanked him and entered the market quarter. She noted the guardsman's name in her memory. A week later she checked the town charter at the guild house. There is no such ordinance. There never was.
> **Age 24 (679 TR)** — A friend — companion, neighbour, or kin — died badly. The loss left a mark.
> **Age 25 (680 TR)** — The mission had been going well — that was the part that was hardest to hear. Twelve people who thought they understood the situation. She filed it under: the thing you assume is working is the thing that kills you.
> **Age 26 (681 TR)** — She knelt and gave her answers. The Hesa-Eran bound her to the order. She had known since she was twelve it would come to this.
> **Age 27 (682 TR)** — She studied from a written wordlist compiled by someone who had died at Osel, supplemented by conversations with three different informants who disagreed on half the vocabulary. The disagreements were the most informative part. Language is politics in concentrated form.
> **Age 28 (683 TR)** — She ruined two yards of figured cloth that the master had been saving for a specific commission. The sound the mistress made when she saw it was not a word. She sat with that sound for a long time afterward — not out of guilt exactly, but out of the specific determination to understand precisely what had gone wrong at every stage of the decision and execution so that she could guarantee she never made those specific errors again. She can still describe all six errors in sequence. She will never make any of them.
> **Age 29 (684 TR)** — She stood somewhere and saw something beautiful. She kept the image of it longer than most things.
> **Age 30 (685 TR)** — She stayed up until the candle was gone. She was not sure what she had been doing for the last hour.
> **Age 31 (686 TR)** — She was who the village came to when there was no one else.
> **Age 32 (687 TR)** — He endured. The details were different; the shape was the same.
> **Age 33 (688 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 34 (689 TR)** — Matakea. The full title. She had spent seven years as Ashesa working toward this and the ceremony itself was over in an afternoon.
> **Age 35 (690 TR)** — Rumour or accusation — fair or unfair — put her standing at risk.
> **Age 36 (691 TR)** — She began writing — not because anyone asked, but because it needed doing.
> **Age 37 (692 TR)** — She stopped at a familiar door and stood on the threshold. She could not have said what made her hesitate. She went in.
> **Age 38 (693 TR)** — Months of close reading: liturgical texts, canonical precedent, the letters of the senior clergy. The work was slow and the illumination on the margins of the oldest manuscripts was occasionally distracting.
> **Age 39 (694 TR)** — A woman she knew was killed — a neighbour, a trader she dealt with, someone whose face she knew. It was the kind of death that made everyone quieter for a while.
> **Age 40 (695 TR)** — She woke knowing what she needed to do. She had gone to sleep with no idea. She did not question it.
> **Age 41 (696 TR)** — A remote posting: a hamlet on the edge of the kingdom, or a lord’s estate where the last priest had left without explanation. The people needed the rites performed and she performed them.
> **Age 42 (697 TR)** — The other apprentice got the easier tasks, the better hours, the master's patience. She got the corrections and the night work and the jobs that were beneath notice if they went right and humiliating if they went wrong. She understood that this was partly preference and partly a test — the masters who treat their female apprentices harder are testing whether they will leave, because the easy ones always leave and the hard ones stay and become the best practitioners. She stayed. She is still deciding whether the theory justifies the practice.
> **Age 43 (698 TR)** — He endured. The details were different; the shape was the same.
> **Age 44 (699 TR)** — Patronage came with influence and obligations. She accepted both and managed the latter with care.
> **Age 45 (700 TR)** — She watched the mistress take apart a piece she had thought was finished and rebuild the central section from scratch in forty minutes. It was the same result, technically — the dimensions matched, the specifications were met. But the new version had something the first version had not had, something she could see when they were side by side but cannot yet name precisely. The mistress saw her looking. She did not explain. She said: when you can say what the difference is, you are ready for the examination.
> **Age 46 (701 TR)** — Her childbearing years had passed. She felt the change in herself, and adapted.
> **Age 47 (702 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 49 (704 TR)** — She was called upon to speak where women rarely did — and was not found wanting.
> **Age 50 (705 TR)** — She completed the commission and the merchant accepted it and paid the full rate. The merchant did not know she was the apprentice rather than the journeyman. She decided not to clarify this, which is an ethical position she has revisited several times since. Her conclusion: the work was good enough. The classification of who did it is a guild matter, not a customer matter. She told the master. The master said: correct.
> **Age 51 (706 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 52 (707 TR)** — She chose to end the enmity. It cost her pride and gained her something she could not name. Reconciled with Voran
> **Age 53 (708 TR)** — The approach was made in person, by a woman she had never seen, who knew things about her that no one had any business knowing. The price was stated plainly. She paid it. She is still paying it. She has not yet determined who is behind the approach, which tells her they are very good at this.
> **Age 54 (709 TR)** — The death of a relative brought unexpected property or coin into her hands.
> **Age 55 (710 TR)** — She was ill enough to frighten the household. She recovered, though the recovery took longer than the illness. The illness left a lasting mark: stamina −1.
> **Age 56 (711 TR)** — She noticed the lightness at her belt on the way home and knew. A market crowd, a moment of distraction. She had been careful. It had not been enough. She counted what she still had left and did not dwell on it.
> **Age 57 (712 TR)** — Crisis drew her back to her faith with new fervour.
> **Age 58 (713 TR)** — The Ebasethe arrived before dawn and stayed until the crisis passed. She had sent for her because the midwife said to. She had not expected it to matter. The child was alive. She had a different understanding of the Peonian church after that — less abstract, more personal.
> **Age 59 (714 TR)** — She gave to the church or the poor. It mattered to her that it was done.
> **Age 60 (715 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 61 (716 TR)** — She had managed the household, the accounts, the children's marriages, and the succession of a substantial property for four decades, and she stopped. What she found on the other side of it was time. She had forgotten what time felt like when it belonged to her. She is still learning.
> **Age 62 (717 TR)** — She lit a candle for someone she had not thought of in years. She did not examine this.
> **Age 63 (718 TR)** — Crisis drew her back to her faith with new fervour.
> **Age 64 (719 TR)** — The year passed much as the last had.
> **Age 65 (720 TR)** — She set her affairs in order with the same methodical care she had brought to everything. She felt lighter for it.
