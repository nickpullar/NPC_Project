> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (705 TR)** — The master put her hands on the material and showed her how the grain ran under the surface — not visually, by feel. She had been reading grain by eye. Reading it by touch produces different information. The two readings together produce a third kind of knowledge that she did not have before. She has been running her hands over every piece of material she touches since that afternoon, building the vocabulary.
> **Age 19 (706 TR)** — She has been waiting for three days for the master to look at the commission design she submitted. The master has been busy. She understands this intellectually. The piece is good — she knows the piece is good — and she is waiting to find out whether knowing it is good and being told it is good are the same thing. They are not the same thing, she has learned. Being told produces a different kind of certainty.
> **Age 20 (707 TR)** — The mistress cuffed her across the back of the head hard enough to make her ears ring. The reason was a miscounted measure that had ruined two yards of good cloth. She did not cry — she had learned by this point that crying produced worse outcomes than silence. She fixed the count, restarted the section, and did not make the same error again. She has thought about the incident since mostly in terms of what the mistress actually communicated: that the cloth mattered more than the learning, and that the learning was her problem to solve, not the mistress's problem to manage.
> **Age 21 (708 TR)** — The other apprentice got the easier tasks, the better hours, the master's patience. She got the corrections and the night work and the jobs that were beneath notice if they went right and humiliating if they went wrong. She understood that this was partly preference and partly a test — the masters who treat their female apprentices harder are testing whether they will leave, because the easy ones always leave and the hard ones stay and become the best practitioners. She stayed. She is still deciding whether the theory justifies the practice.
> **Age 22 (709 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 23 (710 TR)** — She heard the cry and had the choice that women always have in these moments: whether joining would help or whether she would be more useful as a witness, which is a calculation she makes in approximately half a second. She positioned herself where she could see the man being pursued, watched where he went, and when the officers arrived she described his route and his description clearly and they found him two streets over. She was thanked by the bailiff. She has been a useful witness in two similar situations. She considers this a form of civic participation she is good at.
> **Age 24 (711 TR)** — The morning begins with whatever the master left unfinished, which is the rule at every workshop and the reason early mornings in every workshop smell the same. She has been doing this for four years. She knows the rhythm of the place — which tasks come in sequence, which the master trusts her with unsupervised, which she is still not permitted to touch. The permitted list grows slowly. She measures her progress by what she is allowed to touch alone.
> **Age 25 (712 TR)** — The magistrate heard her case and found against her, which surprised her until she learned what the other party had paid. She had known in the abstract that the law was purchasable in this city. She knew it differently after that.
> **Age 26 (713 TR)** — She found someone willing to teach and gave that person her full attention. Studied under Elric Chelkar
> **Age 27 (714 TR)** — A man of means noticed her. What followed was not love, precisely, but it was not nothing either. She had a room that was warm and food that was regular, and she understood the terms.
> **Age 28 (715 TR)** — She struck up an acquaintance that would prove valuable in years to come. Made contact: Kelan Weaver (artisan)
> **Age 29 (716 TR)** — She grows what she can in the yard and buys the rest from three specific suppliers whose quality she has tested and trusts. The fourth supplier she tried was cheaper and consistently substandard — she tested three batches before concluding the variance was not batch quality but supplier quality. She has a mental map of the supply chain for every material she uses, who she trusts at each point and for what, and what to do when a source fails. This is most of the craft, she has concluded: the material before it reaches your hands.
> **Age 30 (717 TR)** — She has remade this join four times. The fourth time it is correct by every technical standard she can apply. She is sitting with it and it is still not right and she cannot say why. This is the part of the craft that she cannot explain to anyone who doesn't practise it: the knowledge that something is wrong before she can name what is wrong. She names it eventually, always. She is waiting for the name.
> **Age 31 (718 TR)** — The year passed much as the last had.
> **Age 32 (719 TR)** — He made it legitimate. She had not let herself believe he would. The wedding was modest and the gossip was fierce and she did not care about either.
> **Age 33 (720 TR)** — Her first child arrived. Halden Glazier. She had not understood, until the moment it was placed in her arms, how much of herself she would give to it. week 40 Halden Glazier born (male)
