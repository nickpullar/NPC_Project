> [!background] Background
> **Age 18 (697 TR)** — A secret entrusted to her found its way to those who would pay for it.
> **Age 19 (698 TR)** — She was asked to deliver a package to a specific house in a town sixty miles out and note, on the way back, what she could observe about the movement of men and horses on the Tashal road. She delivered the package. She observed the movement. Her report contained three pieces of information her officer had not expected, which she knows because of how he reacted to those three pieces. She is aware she is useful in a specific way. She is deciding what to do with that awareness.
> **Age 20 (699 TR)** — The camp economy runs on the sutlers and everyone knows it, including the officers who would prefer not to acknowledge it. She extends credit carefully — who is reliable, who has family money, who is the kind of man who runs when things go wrong. She has been cheated precisely twice and recovered from both by knowing which sergeant to speak to. She is carrying the wages of four dead men in her ledger, which she will attempt to recover from their estates when the campaign ends, and which she is fairly certain she will not recover.
> **Age 21 (700 TR)** — There is a specific kind of problem that comes with being a woman in a company that has not fully decided how to handle women in a company. She handled it. She is not going to describe in detail how she handled it because the details are not the point — the point is that she handled it in a way that did not require the sergeant's intervention and did not damage her operational standing and produced a working arrangement with the person involved that, while not friendly, is functional. She has added this to her list of skills that are not in any training manual.
> **Age 22 (701 TR)** — The morning was so misty that she could see only a few steps ahead. She walked slowly and arrived late and was glad of both.
> **Age 23 (702 TR)** — She was ill enough to frighten the household. She recovered, though the recovery took longer than the illness. The illness left a lasting mark: stamina −1.
> **Age 24 (703 TR)** — She came back to herself slowly, and not entirely the same self that had been taken down.
> **Age 25 (704 TR)** — There was a specific moment — she can identify it — when she did something in front of enough witnesses that the question of whether she belonged there stopped being asked. Not permanently, not universally; the question is never permanently retired. But in that unit, in that company, among those particular men, there was a before and an after. She did not celebrate. She did the next thing, and the thing after that. She is aware that the bar was set differently for her than for the men around her and that clearing it was not the same as having it set fairly. She cleared it anyway.
> **Age 26 (705 TR)** — The fever broke on the fourth day. The Esolani who had been attending her — a woman her own age, mild-faced, entirely competent — told her she was past the worst and brought broth. She had not expected to survive. She had a different relationship with the Peonian church after that.
> **Age 27 (706 TR)** — She kept the household alive through ingenuity and stubbornness.
> **Age 28 (707 TR)** — He endured. The details were different; the shape was the same.
> **Age 29 (708 TR)** — She performed an old ritual her grandmother had taught her. She felt better afterwards and did not explain it.
> **Age 30 (709 TR)** — When the coin was offered, she found reasons to look the other way.
> **Age 31 (710 TR)** — She wrote off four debts this campaign. Two dead, one deserted, one simply gone. She adjusts the prices for everyone else accordingly, which is the honest way to run a sutler's operation: the cost of unrecoverable debt is distributed across the credit that does get repaid. She does not complain about this. It is a known cost of the trade. She does note the names carefully, because some of the deserters end up in other armies, and the road network is smaller than it appears.
> **Age 32 (711 TR)** — She sneezed at the worst possible moment. The ceremony continued. The people nearest her found it difficult to maintain appropriate expressions.
> **Age 33 (712 TR)** — He endured. The details were different; the shape was the same.
> **Age 34 (713 TR)** — A fever kept her bedridden for weeks; she emerged gaunt but alive. The illness left a lasting mark: stamina −1.
> **Age 35 (714 TR)** — She was not trained for battlefield wounds. She was trained for illness, for fever, for childbirth. She adapted in the first campaign and has been adapting since. The military surgeons, when there are any, do not always know more than she does. Sometimes they know less. She does the work that needs doing and has stopped spending energy on the question of whether she is supposed to be the one doing it.
> **Age 36 (715 TR)** — He endured. The details were different; the shape was the same.
> **Age 37 (716 TR)** — Another difficult year. Life continued.
> **Age 38 (717 TR)** — The recovery was long. She measured it in small returns — the walk she could finish, the task she could complete.
> **Age 39 (718 TR)** — She had thought herself a better judge of odds than she was. The coin was gone before she had fully reckoned what she'd risked.
> **Age 40 (719 TR)** — She could tell rain from cloud before most felt the wind shift.
> **Age 41 (720 TR)** — Age made its presence felt. She adapted, but felt the difference. The illness passed without lasting harm.
